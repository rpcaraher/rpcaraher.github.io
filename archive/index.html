About

Ray is a PhD Candidate in the Department of Economics at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. His interests are in labor economics, health economics, and the economics of race and gender. He was a research assistant at the Political Economy Research Institute (PERI) at UMass Amherst and worked on a wide range of projects with PERI faculty including on topics related to climate change, labor market policy, unions, and development aid. His dissertation focuses on using methods from economics to understand the social, health, and economic effects of the overturning of Roe v. Wade. His work has recently been published in World Development. He has also worked as a consultant for the Massachusetts Teachers Association (MTA) and has frequently worked as a Teaching Assistant at UMass Amherst.
